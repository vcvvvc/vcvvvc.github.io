Doubler: A crypto asset appreciation tool based on the martingale strategy

📚What is Doubler?

  • Doubler is a Defi tool, which is a “Buff” public product for crypto assets. The product is inspired by the concept of “martingale”, a traditional martingale strategy that requires a large amount of money to support the continuous growth of the betting amount. Doubler solves this problem by crowdfunding and incentivizing a portion of the profits.

Doubler is a very creative and forward-looking DEFI tool that forms a mutually beneficial relationship between users through crowdfunding and incentives, allowing users to share risks and rewards, and increase user confidence in participating in crypto asset investment. If you hold BTC but don’t want to sell it, but worry about the losses caused by the decline, just join Doubler.

I shared my summary in Doubler in this article.

Two Key Features of Doubler

  1. Protection of Asset Value

    The working principle of Doubler is that users first need to choose a trading pair, such as BTC/USDT, and then enter a target price, such as 60000 USDT. If the current price is lower than the target price, users can initiate a Doubler project, set an initial bet amount, such as 100 USDT, and a doubling coefficient, such as 2. Then users need to pay a certain percentage of the fee, such as 10%, as an incentive. In this way, users can post their own projects on the Doubler platform and wait for other users to participate.

  2. High Reward Mechanism

    In the investment of Doubler, you can not only get the benefits of BTC appreciation, but also get DBR rewards. If you are the ultimate winner of this pool, you will get a lot of rewards.

About Reward

In order to motivate the users in the market to join the created mining pool, Doubler will allocate a part of the profit as a reward to the last batch of users who join the mining pool, ensuring that the mining pool runs continuously according to the set parameters. When the capacity of a Layer is less than X times the initial injection amount of the Pool, the providers of the last 50% BTC quantity of the settlement layer will get all the profits. When the capacity of a Layer exceeds X times the initial injection amount of the Pool, no matter how the capacity of the subsequent Layer increases, the BTC providers who join will get the profit of the last layer according to X times the initial injection amount. The profit is distributed according to the weighted average method (X is the multiplier set by the creator).

How to create a pool in Doubler

  • A: Unit Size: Sets the minimum participation quantity for the pool, where participants can only join the pool in multiples of the unit size.
  • B: Initial Injection: The initial amount of cryptocurrency injected into the pool at an early stage.
  • C: Doubling: When the price drop threshold is triggered, it determines the capacity of the next level, ranging from 2X to 5X.
  • D: Fall Rate: Sets the threshold for triggering the next level after a price drop.

Once a user creates a Pool, the smart contract will use Chainlink to obtain the current price of BTC and record it as the cost price. When the price of BTC falls below the threshold set by the user during Pool creation, the Pool will be opened for other users to deposit BTC.

We regard the pool as a whole, if BTC continues to fall, but with the deposit of low-cost BTC, the average price of BTC in the pool will also drop, according to Doubler’s rules, the average price will approach the current price infinitely, when BTC price reaches the target set by the initiator, the pool will terminate and settle through smart contract.

About Doubler

Doubler is an open-source financial gain strategy protocol based on smart contracts, aiming to optimize cryptocurrency asset investment strategies, mitigate risks, and pursue rapid profits through the application of the “martingale” strategy. Drawing inspiration from the concept of “martingale” in financial markets, Doubler leverages market aggregation effects to execute strategies while continuously lowering the cost basis of the capital pool.
